üîä Screen Reader Compatibility

TherapyConnect is compatible with the following screen readers and assistive technologies:

  • JAWS (Job Access With Speech) - Windows
  • NVDA (NonVisual Desktop Access) - Windows
  • VoiceOver - macOS, iOS, and iPadOS
  • TalkBack - Android
  • Narrator - Windows
  • ChromeVox - Chrome OS

Our platform includes proper ARIA labels, semantic HTML, and descriptive alt text to ensure screen reader users can navigate and understand all content effectively.

‚å®Ô∏è Keyboard Navigation

All functionality on TherapyConnect can be accessed using a keyboard alone, without requiring a mouse:

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Tab - Navigate forward through interactive elements
  • Shift + Tab - Navigate backward through interactive elements
  • Enter / Space - Activate buttons and links
  • Arrow Keys - Navigate within menus, tabs, and lists
  • Escape - Close modals and dropdown menus
  • Alt + H - Navigate to homepage
  • Alt + S - Navigate to sessions
  • Alt + M - Navigate to messages

üí° Tip: Press Tab to see focus indicators highlighting interactive elements as you navigate through the page. All focus indicators are clearly visible with high contrast.

üìπ Video & Audio Session Accessibility

Our therapy sessions are designed to be accessible to users with various needs:

Available Accommodations

  • Live Captions: Real-time captioning available for all video sessions
  • Audio-Only Option: Phone sessions available for users who prefer or require audio-only
  • Screen Reader Support: Video interface fully compatible with screen readers

Requesting Accommodations

If you require specific accommodations for your therapy sessions (such as ASL interpretation, extended session times, or specialized communication tools), please contact us at least 48 hours in advance at accessibility@therapyconnect.com.

‚ö†Ô∏è Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, we acknowledge the following limitations we are actively working to address:

  • Some third-party integrated content may not fully meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
  • Certain complex data visualizations in progress tracking may require alternative text descriptions

  • Some older PDF resources in our library may not be fully screen-reader accessible (we're converting these to accessible formats)

We are committed to resolving these issues and regularly audit our platform for accessibility improvements.
